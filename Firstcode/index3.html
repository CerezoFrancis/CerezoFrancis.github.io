CALL OF DUTY
(2003)
Released on October 29, 2003, by Infinity Ward and Activision, the original "Call of Duty" revolutionized the first-person shooter genre with its immersive World War II setting, focusing on American, British, and Soviet soldiers. The game introduced innovative squad-based combat, realistic wartime environments, and a health pack system, enhancing the cinematic experience. Players participated in iconic battles such as the D-Day invasion and the Battle of Stalingrad. With its compelling narrative, groundbreaking gameplay, and successful multiplayer modes, "Call of Duty" received critical acclaim and numerous Game of the Year awards, establishing a legacy that paved the way for one of the most successful franchises in gaming history.
CALL OF DUTY 4: MODERN WARFARE
(2007)
Released on November 5, 2007, "Call of Duty 4: Modern Warfare" by Infinity Ward and Activision revolutionized the gaming world. This iconic game thrust players into intense modern-day conflicts with explosive action and unforgettable missions. Its groundbreaking multiplayer mode and gripping single-player campaign, featuring characters like Captain Price and Soap MacTavish, set new standards for online shooters. With stunning graphics and heart-pounding gameplay, "Modern Warfare" captivated gamers worldwide, becoming a genre-defining masterpiece.
CALL OF DUTY: BLACK OPS II
(2012)
Released on November 13, 2012, "Call of Duty: Black Ops II" by Treyarch and Activision electrified the franchise with groundbreaking innovations. This thrilling sequel unleashed branching storylines, intense futuristic warfare, and a gripping campaign spanning the Cold War to 2025. Packed with strategic gameplay, exhilarating multiplayer modes, and the wildly popular Zombies mode, "Black Ops II" delivered an adrenaline-pumping experience that captivated players and redefined the series.
